Riley Soto presents: Fighting Fascists

For when you want to do things electronically,
but don't like private ownership of the means of production.

In 1958 researchers at Moscow State University developed Setun.
Named after the river in Moscow nearby the university, it was a balanced ternary computer.
Meaning that instead of 0s and 1s as binary computers do, it used 1, 0, and -1.
It was cheaper and faster than state of the art binary computers in the West.
It was, effectively, a decade ahead of binary equivalents..

"but it didn't catch on!"

Yeah, I know, right? That's what I said.
Turns out the Soviets didn't care too much
and instead let the projet die as the state stopped funding.
Talk about missed opportunities!

A couple decades later another experiment with ternary computers,
named "Setun II", was built in Moscow State University.
Unfortunately, once again, the project ran out of funding,
but initial constructions were just as promising...
